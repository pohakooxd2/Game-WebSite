      What is a Battle Royale game?

      Battle royale games are a mix of shooting and survival, the goal will be to be the last player alive at the end of the game to achieve victory. Battle royale games typically take place on large maps where dozens of players will have to struggle to stay alive. The more time passes, the smaller the playing area will be, forcing players to meet and compete. It will therefore be necessary to find the right balance between staying alive and eliminating your opponents to be able to stay in the game as long as possible. For this you will be able to find equipment and weapons of different qualities on the map, the more the game progresses the more powerful your character will be thanks to the equipment collected. If you kill another player you will be able to recover all of the equipment they were carrying.

      What is the origin of Battle Royale games?

      The term Battle Royale comes from the Japanese novel of the same name which appeared in 1999 and was adapted into a film in 2000. The Hunger Games saga made the genre popular and developed the concept of “last man standing”. In video games the concept materialized with the DayZ Mode of Arma 2 released in 2013. Subsequently Brendan Greene nicknamed the player "PlayerUnknown" developed a mode on this principle for Arma 3 called PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds. The well-known H1Z1: King of the Kill, Apex Legend and the most famous of all Fortnite followed which made the genre popular with the general public and met with immense success. Battle Royale games then became a reference in the world of video games and many free titles were born on browsers in particular, playable directly without downloading.
